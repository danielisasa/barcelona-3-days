Before You Go

Essential
Travel Tips

🏨
Where to Stay
The Caledonian is right in the city center with great value. Other excellent options: H10 Art Gallery, Hotel Nouvel, Acta Splendid, Hostal la Palmera.
✈️
Airport to City
From El Prat Airport you can reach the center by train, bus, metro, taxi, or private transfer. The Aerobús is fast and drops you at Plaça Catalunya.
🎫
Barcelona Pass
If visiting many monuments, the Barcelona Pass saves money and lets you skip queues. The Hola BCN! card gives unlimited public transport for 2–5 days.
🚇
Getting Around
The T-Casual card (10 trips) works on metro, bus, and tram. The city is also very walkable — many top sights are within strolling distance of each other.
💶
Free Museum Days
Picasso Museum is free Thursday afternoons. Hospital Sant Pau is free on the 1st Sunday of each month. MNAC is free on Saturday afternoons.
🎟️
Book in Advance
Sagrada Família, Park Güell, Casa Batlló, and La Pedrera all sell out. Book tickets online at least a few days ahead to guarantee entry and skip the queues.
1
Day One

Modernisme, Gràcia & the Gothic Quarter

9:00 AM · Morning
Sagrada Família
Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece and one of the world's most extraordinary buildings. Take the metro (lines L2 or L5) and arrive early. The interior — with its breathtaking stained glass — is worth every minute inside.
⚠️ Book tickets online days in advance. A fixed number are sold daily and they sell out fast.
11:00 AM
Hospital de la Santa Creu i Sant Pau
A short walk from the Sagrada Família, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the jewels of Catalan Modernisme — a surreal complex of Modernista pavilions surrounded by gardens.
12:30 PM
Park Güell
Head to the most beautiful park in the city. Gaudí's colorful mosaic terraces offer sweeping views over Barcelona. The monumental zone now requires paid entry with a daily capacity limit.
💡 Book the monumental zone ticket well ahead — it regularly sells out.
2:30 PM · Lunch + Afternoon
Neighborhood of Gràcia
Walk down from Park Güell into one of Barcelona's most enchanting neighborhoods. Explore its narrow streets, local boutiques, and charming plazas: Virreina, del Sol, de la Revolució, del Diamant. Don't miss Casa Vicens — Gaudí's very first major work.
🍽️ For tapas: Intrèpid de Gràcia or Bar Bodega Quimet are local favorites.
5:00 PM
Gothic Quarter
Take the metro (line 3) to Plaça Catalunya and head into the Gothic Quarter — the medieval heart of Barcelona. Wander through its atmospheric lanes to the Cathedral, Temple of Augustus, Carrer del Bisbe, Plaça de Sant Felip Neri, and the spectacular Plaça Reial.
7:00 PM · Evening
Las Ramblas & La Boqueria Market
Stroll the full length of Las Ramblas to the Columbus Monument, stepping inside the famous La Boqueria market to taste and browse. Find the Joan Miró mosaic underfoot near the midpoint of the boulevard.
🎭 End the night with a flamenco show at Los Tarantos, right on Plaça Reial — a perfect Barcelona finale.
"Barcelona is a place where you feel the city wants you to be happy."

Day Two

Passeig de Gràcia, El Born & The Bunkers

9:00 AM · Morning
La Pedrera (Casa Milà)
Begin on the Passeig de Gràcia — Barcelona's grandest boulevard — with Gaudí's undulating apartment building, La Pedrera. The rooftop terrace with its warrior chimney sculptures is otherworldly. Arrive early or book ahead to avoid the queue.
11:00 AM
Casa Batlló
A few steps down the boulevard, Casa Batlló is one of the most beautiful buildings on Earth. The building's dragon-like roof, bone-shaped columns, and luminous interior are pure genius. The interactive audio guide included with the ticket is particularly engaging.
💡 Walk the whole length of the Passeig de Gràcia to admire Casa Amatller, Casa Mulleras, and the other Modernista façades — the so-called "Block of Discord."
12:30 PM
Palau de la Música Catalana
A UNESCO World Heritage concert hall and a dazzling explosion of stained glass, mosaics, and sculpted stone. Visit via a guided tour or by attending one of its concerts.
2:00 PM · Lunch
Lunch near El Born
After the Palau de la Música, stop for lunch nearby. Tosca Palau and Casa Lolea are both excellent options in the area.
3:30 PM · Afternoon
El Born Neighborhood
A medieval neighborhood packed with culture. Explore the magnificent Gothic basilica of Santa María del Mar, the old iron Mercat del Born, the colorful Mercat de Santa Catarina, the Carrer Montcada, and the Parc de la Ciutadella — Barcelona's green lung.

6:00 PM · Sunset
Bunkers del Carmel
From the Arc de Triomf, take bus V19 to the Ctra. del Carmel – Mühlberg stop, then walk 10 minutes up to the Bunkers — WWII anti-aircraft battery ruins that offer the most breathtaking 360° panorama in Barcelona. Stay for sunset. As darkness falls, the city lights up below you like a living map.
🌅 This is the single best viewpoint in Barcelona. Don't miss it.
3
Day Three

Raval, Barceloneta & Montjuïc

9:30 AM · Morning
El Raval Neighborhood
Start the final day with a relaxed stroll through El Raval, one of Barcelona's oldest and most atmospheric neighborhoods. Walk along Carrer Tallers with its quirky independent shops, and finish at the Rambla del Raval — where Botero's famous bronze Cat sculpture sits in the middle of the boulevard.
10:30 AM
Palau Güell
A short walk from the Rambla, the Palau Güell is one of Gaudí's most remarkable early works — extraordinary for its innovative use of natural light filtering through dome and skylights, and its spectacular ceramic chimney sculptures on the rooftop terrace.
🎫 Book tickets in advance online to guarantee entry.
12:00 PM · Midday
Port Vell & La Barceloneta
Walk down Las Ramblas to the Columbus Monument and the Port Vell — the ancient harbor filled with luxury yachts. Cross the Rambla del Mar to Maremagnum for a sea-view drink, or head to the Time Out Market for lunch with local flavors.
Continue along the seafront promenade to La Barceloneta — the charming fishermen's neighborhood with narrow streets, fine sandy beaches, and excellent restaurants serving fresh fish and paella.
🦞 Best seafood restaurants: La Peninsular and La Cova Fumada.
3:00 PM · Afternoon
Montjuïc by Cable Car
Head back toward Port Vell and take the Telefèric de Montjuïc cable car from Avenida de Miramar up to the mountain top. Pre-booking the ticket is recommended.
3:30 PM
Montjuïc Castle
A defensive fortress with over 300 years of turbulent history — anti-aircraft batteries, cannons, and extraordinary panoramic views of the city and the entire Barcelona coastline from every angle.
5:00 PM
The 1992 Olympic Ring
A short walk from the castle, the Olympic Ring — site of the 1992 Olympic Games — features the Estadio Olímpico Lluís Companys, the Palau Sant Jordi, Bernat Picornell pools, and the iconic Calatrava telecommunications tower.
6:00 PM
MNAC — National Art Museum of Catalonia
The grand National Palace houses one of the world's finest collections of Romanesque art, plus works by Picasso, Velázquez, and Dalí. Even if you skip the museum itself, the two free outdoor terraces offer one of the most spectacular views in all of Barcelona.
9:00 PM · Evening
Font Màgica & Plaça d'Espanya
Ride the escalators down from Montjuïc to the Magic Fountain (Font Màgica). When night falls, a spectacular free show of water, music, and colored light begins — one of the most memorable experiences in Barcelona. Walk between the two Venetian towers to Plaça d'Espanya for dinner nearby.
🎇 The Magic Fountain show is completely free — don't miss it.
